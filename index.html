Model Scope in AngularJS

Under Construction

In Views and Controllers

When Controllers Nest

The nested controller has access to any variables found on the parent controller's scope. It gets weird when you override those things though.

In Forms

According to the docs, forms get their own controller (usually happens behind the scenes). This acts like nested controller scope as described above. A form's scope shouldn't be confused with a form's namespace, which is like a scope for validatable aspects of the form.

ng-repeat

Every repetition in an ng-repeat directive ends up with its own scope, nested in the parent controller's scope.

In Directives

In Directive Controllers

Isolated Scope

The right way to take in or manipulate model data from the document hosting a directive is to give the directive it's own scope. You can then determine how data is marshalled from the hosting controller and the directive's own controller.

Isolated Scope's effect on Sibling Directives

The require property of a directive is used to add other directives as siblings of this one. Unfortunately, the decisions you make around scope on your main directive will effect the scope of any sibling directives. In this example, we try to enable the use of the ng-model attribute in a custom directive. We would normally do this by making our directive a sibling of the out-of-the-box ngModel directive. This fails when we decide to make our directive have an isolated scope. This behavior is described in AngularJS Issue 1924
